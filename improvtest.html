Now let me roughly say how AI Improv works. I'm not going to make the below too polished.

Beginning of session: I get a sense for what the learner(s) is working on today or this week. I listen for problems they are trying to solve or tasks they need to complete.

Then: I say something like "Ok, let's muck around with AI! It's fun, I'll drive first. Do you want to work through [xyz thing they just told me about] or do you have another problem or task in mind and want to see how everyday AI can help?

Then: they pick the task, and then I go into "I do" mode. I pull up my favorite AI product for the task.

Example:
- Participant needs to coach someone they manage on how to work better with a peer where there is conflict. We use an LLM to act as an executive coach, first giving context (describing the situation in some detail), talking about different coaching approaches, and doing role playing where the everyday-AI product acts as either the manager or the report.

- Participant needs to write a project proposal. They struggle with "blank page syndrome" -- getting to a bad first draft is hard, much less a good one. We give context to the everyday-AI tool, and we get to a good first draft within minutes.

- Participant has a day of packed meetings and hasn't had time to review the materials. We feed materials into an everyday AI tool, and get the executive prepared with summaries, questions to ask, suggestions to make.

- Participant gets a lot of emails where they need to say "great job" in some way. It's exhausting and painful to keep up with the emails and respond to them. I pull up an image-gen AI tool to show them how they can have fun by generating images that show their thanks. My go to is a prompt like "give me an image of a beach with rocks on it, and the rocks spell Janet Rocks." Then we do a few more just for fun.

All of the above examples blow people away.

They get to aha and haha moments every single time.

Without fail.
