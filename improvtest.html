AI Improv: A Facilitator's Guide

Introduction to AI Improv

AI Improv is an innovative teaching technique designed to make artificial intelligence accessible and engaging for adults. It combines the principles of improvisation with practical AI tool usage to solve real-world problems or tasks that learners are currently facing. This method focuses on creating a dynamic learning experience where participants can directly see the value and impact of AI in their work and personal lives.

Goal of AI Improv

The primary goal of AI Improv is to demystify AI technology for adults and demonstrate its practical applications through hands-on experience. By leveraging real-life scenarios, learners can overcome the initial intimidation often associated with AI and prompt engineering, fostering a more intuitive and confident approach to using AI tools.

Facilitator's Role

As a facilitator, your role is to guide participants through the AI Improv process, ensuring they not only understand how AI tools work but also recognize their potential to solve everyday challenges. You'll act as a bridge between AI concepts and practical applications, using the "I do, we do, you do" teaching model to gradually transfer responsibility to the learner.

Implementing AI Improv: Step-by-Step

Preparation
- Understand the AI tools and platforms you'll be using.
- Ensure you have access to these tools and that they are set up for the session.

Engage with the Participant(s)
- Start the session by discussing the participants' current tasks or challenges.
- Encourage openness by sharing your enthusiasm for exploring AI solutions.

Select a Task
- Ask participants if they want to address a problem they mentioned or explore a new task where AI could be applied.
- Ensure the task is suitable for demonstrating the capabilities of AI in a meaningful way.

Demonstration ("I do")
- Choose an AI tool relevant to the selected task.
- Explain your thought process as you use the tool to approach the task, demonstrating its application and potential solutions.

Collaborative Practice ("We do")
- Invite participants to join you in using the AI tool, guiding them through the process.
- Encourage questions and exploration, fostering a collaborative learning environment.

Independent Practice ("You do")
- Have participants use the AI tool on their own, applying what they've learned to the task.
- Offer support and feedback, emphasizing exploration and creativity.

Debrief and Reflect
- Discuss the experience, focusing on "aha" and "ha ha" moments.
- Encourage reflection on how AI can be applied in other aspects of their work or life.

Tips for Success

- Emphasize the accessibility and practicality of AI, regardless of technical background.
- Foster an environment of experimentation and fun to engage learners.
- Use real-life, relatable examples that resonate with the participants' experiences.
- Aim for quick wins to demonstrate the immediate value of AI.

Conclusion

AI Improv is a powerful tool in the arsenal of anyone teaching AI. It not only demystifies AI but also empowers learners to envision and implement AI solutions in their daily lives. By following this guide, you'll be equipped to deliver engaging and impactful learning experiences that highlight the transformative potential of AI.
